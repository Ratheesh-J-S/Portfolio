Project information

  • Title:Digital image edge detection
  • Project site: Offsite
  • In this paper, a comparative study is conducted on vector gradient, Sobel, Prewitt, and Canny edge detection techniques. These methods are flexible, leveraging operator calculations and adjustable kernels. For applications requiring structural outlining with limited vertical gradient extension, Sobel and Prewitt yield satisfactory results with threshold adjustments.
  • However, they are vulnerable to noise. The quality of edge detection is highly dependent on standard deviation and threshold values; larger parameters lead to more blurred edges and malfunctions around non-linear edge changes. Canny edge detection, while the most complex, excels in preserving texture and producing continuous edges.
  • Despite its higher computational cost, Canny offers superior detection quality, demonstrating a trade-off between processing time and output clarity. This study highlights the strengths and limitations of each technique, guiding their application based on specific requirements.
